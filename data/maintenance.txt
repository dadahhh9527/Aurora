Robot Vacuum Maintenance & Care

Why maintenance matters
Regular maintenance keeps suction strong, prevents odors, and extends the life of the robot and its consumables. Most performance complaints (weak suction, streaky mopping, short runtime) come from skipped maintenance.

Dust bin
Empty the dust bin after every few cleaning sessions, or daily in homes with pets or heavy debris. A full bin reduces suction and can trigger error warnings. For self-emptying docks, replace the dock dust bag when it is full (typically every 30-60 days).

Filters
Most robots use a foam pre-filter plus a HEPA filter.
- Tap out loose dust after each bin emptying.
- Rinse washable filters with cold water every 2-4 weeks, then air dry completely (24 hours) before reinstalling. Never install a damp filter.
- Replace the HEPA filter every 2-3 months, or sooner if suction drops or allergies worsen.

Main brush (roller)
Hair and fibers wrap around the main brush over time.
- Check weekly; cut away wrapped hair with the included tool or scissors.
- Remove and clean the brush end caps and bearings monthly.
- Replace the main brush every 6-12 months depending on use.

Side brushes
Side brushes bend and wear out.
- Straighten bent bristles by soaking briefly in warm water.
- Clean tangled hair off the side brush monthly.
- Replace every 3-6 months when bristles stay bent or frayed.

Sensors and charging contacts
- Wipe cliff sensors (bottom of the robot) and the laser/camera lens with a dry microfiber cloth weekly. Dust on cliff sensors can make the robot behave as if it sees stairs.
- Clean the metal charging contacts on the robot and dock with a dry cloth to ensure reliable charging.

Wheels
Check the main wheels and caster (front swivel wheel) for hair and debris every few weeks. A clogged caster causes the robot to drag or veer.

Mop pads and water tank (combo models)
- Rinse or machine-wash mop pads after each mopping session; replace fabric pads every 1-3 months.
- Empty the dirty-water tank and rinse the clean-water tank regularly to prevent mildew.
- Descale the water system periodically if you use hard water.
- For docks with auto mop wash, clean the wash tray weekly to prevent buildup and odor.

Battery care
- Keep the robot on the dock when not in use so it stays charged.
- If storing for a long time, charge to around 50% and store in a cool, dry place; recharge every few months.
- Expect gradual capacity loss; replace the battery when runtime drops noticeably (often after 2-3 years).

Recommended maintenance schedule
- After each run: empty bin (pet homes), rinse mop pads.
- Weekly: check main brush and caster, wipe sensors and lens.
- Every 2-4 weeks: rinse filters, clean side brushes.
- Every 2-3 months: replace HEPA filter, inspect side brush.
- Every 6-12 months: replace main brush; assess battery health.
