Robot Vacuum Buying Guide

How to choose the right robot vacuum
Choosing a robot vacuum comes down to your floor type, home size, whether you have pets, and how much manual work you want to avoid. Read the sections below to match a model to your home.

Suction power (Pa)
Suction is measured in pascals (Pa).
- 1500-2000 Pa: fine for hard floors (tile, laminate, hardwood) and light dust in small apartments.
- 2500-4000 Pa: recommended for low-pile carpets and homes with some pet hair.
- 5000 Pa and above: best for medium-pile carpet, heavy pet hair, and larger homes.
Higher suction uses more battery, so balance suction with runtime and noise.

Small apartments (under 70 sq meters / 750 sq ft)
For small apartments, a compact model with 2000-2500 Pa suction, gyroscope or basic LiDAR navigation, and a slim body (under 9.5 cm tall) is usually enough. A large dust bin matters less because runs are short. Look for good edge-cleaning brushes to reach along skirting boards.

Large homes (over 100 sq meters / 1000 sq ft)
For large homes, prioritize LiDAR navigation with multi-floor mapping, a battery of 5000 mAh or more, and automatic recharge-and-resume so the robot returns to finish where it left off. A self-emptying dock reduces how often you touch the dust bin.

Homes with pets
For pet owners, choose a model with a tangle-resistant rubber (or dual) main brush, strong suction (3000 Pa+), a HEPA filter to trap dander, and a sealed dust bin. Anti-tangle brushes greatly reduce hair wrapping around the roller.

Vacuum-only vs vacuum-and-mop combo
- Vacuum-only: simpler, cheaper, best if you rarely mop or have mostly carpet.
- Vacuum-and-mop combo: cleans and mops in one pass. Look for a lifting mop pad (so it lifts on carpet), electronically controlled water flow, and, ideally, a dock that washes and dries the mop pads.
Combo robots are ideal for hard floors like tile and sealed hardwood.

Navigation types
- Random/bump navigation: cheapest, no mapping, inefficient coverage. Fine for a single small room.
- Gyroscope navigation: tidy back-and-forth pattern, decent for small homes.
- LiDAR (laser) navigation: precise real-time mapping, no-go zones, and multi-room/multi-floor maps. Best coverage and the recommended choice for most buyers.
- Camera/vSLAM: good obstacle recognition; performance can drop in very dark rooms.

Dock and maintenance features
- Self-emptying dock: empties the robot's bin into a larger bag, so you handle dust every few weeks instead of daily.
- Auto mop wash and dry: rinses and hot-air dries the mop pads to prevent odor.
- Auto water refill: tops up the robot's clean-water tank from the dock.
These features cost more up front but dramatically cut daily effort.

Budget guidance
- Entry level: reliable vacuuming, basic navigation, manual bin emptying.
- Mid range: LiDAR mapping, app control, combo mopping, larger battery.
- Premium: self-emptying, auto mop wash and dry, obstacle avoidance, multi-floor maps.
Buy the cheapest model that covers your must-have features rather than paying for features you will not use.

Quick recommendations
- Small hard-floor apartment: mid-range combo, 2500 Pa, LiDAR, slim body.
- Large home, no pets: LiDAR with multi-floor maps, 5000 mAh battery, self-emptying dock.
- Pet household: tangle-resistant brush, 3000 Pa+, HEPA filter, self-emptying dock.
- Mostly carpet: high suction (5000 Pa+), carpet boost, avoid heavy mopping features.
